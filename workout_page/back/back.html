Back Breakdown

Strengthen your traps, rhomboids, lats and lower back with rows, pull-ups and deadlifts — organized by muscle region and difficulty.

For best results, perform each exercise for 3–4 sets of 8, 10, 12, 15 or 20 reps depending on your fitness level and goals.

Upper Back (Trapezius)

Beginner
  • Barbell Shrugs
  • Dumbbell Shrugs
  • Face Pulls
  • Farmer's Walks
Intermediate
  • Upright Rows
  • Behind-the-Back Barbell Shrugs
Advanced
  • Snatch Grip High Pulls

Mid Back (Rhomboids, Middle Trapezius)

Beginner
  • Bent-Over Rows
  • Seated Cable Rows
  • Inverted Rows
  • Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows
  • Chest-Supported Rows
  • Machine Rows
Intermediate
  • T-Bar Rows
Advanced
  • Meadows Rows

Lats

Beginner
  • Lat Pulldowns
  • Straight-Arm Pulldowns
  • Dumbbell Pullovers
  • Assisted Pull-Ups
Intermediate
  • Pull-Ups
  • Single-Arm Lat Pulldowns
  • Rack Chins
Advanced
  • Pendlay Rows

Lower Back (Erector Spinae)

Beginner
  • Back Extensions
  • Superman Exercise
  • Bird-Dog Exercise
Intermediate
  • Deadlifts
  • Good Mornings
  • Reverse Hyperextensions
  • Seated Good Mornings
  • Romanian Deadlifts
