Daily Meal Plan

Timed for Training

Early Morning · 7:00 AM

Wake-Up Fuel

  • 1 glass warm water with lemon
  • Black coffee or green tea
  • Optional: 5 g creatine if supplementing
Breakfast · 8:00–8:30 AM

Two Options

  • Option 1: 4 egg whites + 2 whole eggs (boiled or scrambled), 1 multigrain toast or small oats roti, 1 small bowl of oats with skim milk + chia seeds + berries (or banana slices), 5 soaked almonds + 2 walnuts
  • Option 2 (vegetarian): 2 moong dal cheelas with paneer filling, 1 small bowl low-fat curd, herbal tea or black coffee
Pre-Workout · 10:30–11:00 AM

Quick Energy

  • 1 banana or apple
  • 2 eggs
Post-Workout · 1:00 PM

Recovery Window

  • 1 scoop whey protein in water (within 30 minutes of your workout)
Lunch · 1:30–2:00 PM

Main Meal

  • 1 cup brown rice or 2 small whole wheat rotis
  • Grilled chicken breast, paneer (100–150 g) or tofu
  • 1 cup cooked dal or rajma
  • Salad (cucumber, carrot, tomato, onion)
  • 1 tsp ghee for healthy fat
Evening Snack · 5:00 PM

Three Options

  • Option 1: Roasted chana + green tea
  • Option 2: Low-fat paneer cubes (100 g) with black pepper + lemon
  • Option 3: 1 scoop whey protein
Dinner · 7:30–8:00 PM

Lean & Green

  • 2 small multigrain rotis or 1 cup quinoa
  • Grilled chicken, fish, tofu or paneer (100–150 g)
  • Stir-fried veggies in olive oil (broccoli, bell peppers, beans, carrots, zucchini)
Bedtime · 9:30–10:00 PM

Overnight Recovery

  • 1 cup low-fat milk or 100 g Greek yogurt
  • Optional: add 1 tsp flaxseed powder
Train to Match

Weekly Tips

Strength Training

Train 4–5x per week with a focus on compound lifts — squats, deadlifts and bench press.

Cardio

2–3x per week — HIIT or brisk walking for 20–30 minutes.

Hydration

Drink 3+ litres of water every day to support performance and recovery.

Cheat Meal

One cheat meal per week is fine — keep it to a meal, not a full day.
